# Product Vision

## Product Purpose

The app helps one individual user build and maintain routines, organize personal tasks, and recover from interruptions without being punished by rigid streak mechanics.

The product focuses on reducing the gap between knowing what should be done and actually doing it.

## Core User Problem

The user may know which routines and tasks are important but still struggle with:

- starting consistently,
- maintaining routines over time,
- returning after missed days,
- keeping personal organization simple,
- seeing meaningful long-term progress,
- staying motivated when energy or focus is low.

Traditional todo apps mainly organize tasks. Traditional habit trackers often focus too strongly on streak preservation. This app combines both areas while keeping routines as the primary product focus.

## Product Promise

The app does not demand perfection.

It helps the user take the next realistic step, build momentum, and continue after interruptions.

## Target User

The initial product is designed for one individual using the app privately on one Android device.

The MVP is not social and does not include comparison, leaderboards, shared routines, public profiles, or competitive features.

## Desired Behavior Change

The app should help the user:

- build useful routines,
- complete routines with minimal interaction,
- organize personal tasks alongside routines,
- maintain consistency without fear-based streak pressure,
- recover quickly after missed days,
- see personal progress over longer periods,
- feel motivated by short, polished feedback.

## Product Principles

The app should be:

- encouraging without being childish,
- motivating without being manipulative,
- visually polished without becoming distracting,
- structured without becoming overwhelming,
- private by default,
- usable offline,
- simple for common actions,
- respectful of missed days,
- technically prepared for future iOS support without implementing iOS in the MVP.

## Non-Goals for the MVP

The MVP is not:

- a social network,
- a team project-management tool,
- a calendar replacement,
- a complex role-playing game,
- a medical or health application,
- an AI life coach,
- a cloud-first product,
- an iOS release.
